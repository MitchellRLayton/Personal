Experience


I’ve been proactive at finding experience to bolster my academic resume through positions at the California Energy Commission and the Pacific Gas and Electric company (PG&E). Both positions held during the respective summers of 2017 and 2018 were congruent with the skills that I have been learning in university. Developing complex SQL queries and procedures to fulfill business requirements was the first major skill I developed at the Energy Commission—it was the first experience I had in developing structured database schemas in a relational setting. This job provided me with a concrete understanding of data manipulation, aggregation, segmentation, and transformation within SQL databases. These skills were absorbed well as they served as my foundational understanding to programming and data processes before I partook in my primary data science coursework. Such coursework involved a heavy usage of both the Python and R programming languages to serve as my primary toolkit for scripting, data collection & visualization, and statistical/machine learning projects.

The last and most recent experience I had was at PG&E in San Francisco—working among a team that was responsible for the collection and reporting of crucial company metrics. Their biggest need was to create a new systematic approach to data collection and reporting from the many different lines of businesses within a very large PG&E company. This position offered me an inclusive and thorough summer project: I learned of their systematic function and role within the company, brainstormed, researched, and provided insight towards new possible approaches, and carried out the development of a prototyped web application. Major skills that I developed throughout this internship were team communication, developing a web application using frameworks (particularly Django & ASP.NET MVC), and more nuanced skills like HTML, CSS, Javascript, and relational database schema.
